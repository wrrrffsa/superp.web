Philippine Festivals

Sinulog Festival

The Sinulog Festival is a religious and cultural festival held annually in Cebu City. It honors the Santo Niño, or the child Jesus. The festival features a grand parade with dancers wearing colorful costumes, as well as street parties and concerts.

Ati-Atihan Festival

The Ati-Atihan Festival is another religious and cultural festival, held in Aklan. It celebrates the arrival of the Malay people to the Philippines, as well as the Santo Niño. The festival features street dancing and parades, with performers wearing black body paint and colorful costumes.

Dinagyang Festival

The Dinagyang Festival is a religious and cultural festival held in Iloilo City. It honors the Santo Nino, as well as the arrival of Malay settlers. The festival features street dances and competitions, as well as a fluvial procession and a solemn procession.

Pahiyas Festival

The Pahiyas Festival is a harvest festival held in Lucban, Quezon. It is known for its colorful decorations made of kiping, or rice wafers, as well as fruits, vegetables, and other agricultural products. The festival also features street dancing, a procession, and a cooking contest.

Kadayawan Festival

The Kadayawan Festival is a thanksgiving festival held in Davao City to celebrate the bountiful harvests and diverse cultures of the region. It features street dancing, floral floats, and a variety of competitions, including the Indak-Indak sa Kadalanan, a street dance competition featuring participants in colorful costumes and headdresses.

Panagbenga Festival

The Panagbenga Festival is a flower festival held in Baguio City to celebrate the season of blooming flowers. The festival features a grand parade of floats adorned with colorful flowers, as well as street dancing performances by participants in floral costumes. It is held every February and is a major tourist attraction in the city.

MassKara Festival

The MassKara Festival is a festival held in Bacolod City to celebrate the resilience and festive spirit of the Negrenses. It features street dancing competitions with participants wearing colorful masks and costumes adorned with beads, feathers, and sequins. The festival also includes food fairs, concerts, and a beauty pageant.

Malatarlak Festival

The Malatarlak Festival (or Melting Pot Festival), celebrated every January in Tarlac City, is one of the most remarkable festivals in the province. In 2011, the City Mayor then changed the name of the festival to Melting Pot Festival, but it is still remembered by its former name.

Malatarlak Festival

The Moriones is a lenten festival held annually on Holy Week on the island of Marinduque, Philippines. The "Moriones" are men and women in costumes and masks replicating the garb of biblical Imperial Roman soldiers as interpreted by locals. The Moriones tradition has inspired the creation of other festivals in the Philippines where cultural practices is turned into street festivals.
